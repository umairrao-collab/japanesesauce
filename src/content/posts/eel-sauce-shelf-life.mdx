Homemade eel sauce keeps for **about 2 weeks in the fridge**, while store-bought lasts far longer thanks to preservatives. Here is exactly how to store it and how to tell when it has turned.

## Quick answer

<Callout type="tip">
  Store eel sauce in an airtight glass jar in the coldest part of your fridge (not the door). Homemade: ~2 weeks. Opened store-bought: 3–6 months. Unopened store-bought: check the printed best-by date.
</Callout>

## How long it lasts by storage method

| Storage | Homemade | Store-bought (opened) |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Fridge | ~2 weeks | 3–6 months |
| Freezer | 3 months | Not recommended |
| Pantry | Not safe | Until best-by (unopened) |

## Signs your eel sauce has gone bad

- Sour or off smell
- Mold or cloudiness
- Fermented, fizzy texture

If you spot any of these, throw it out.
